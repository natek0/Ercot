The optimal dispatch rule (from Part IV.3)

Let \(\mu\) be the marginal value of stored charge (the "water value"), \(P_t\) the price now, \(\eta_c,\eta_d\) the charge/discharge efficiencies, and \(c_{\text{deg}}\) the wear cost. The optimal policy is a price band:

\[ \text{sell (discharge) if}\qquad P_t \;>\; \frac{\mu}{\eta_d} + c_{\text{deg}} \] \[ \text{buy (charge) if}\qquad \mu \;>\; \frac{P_t + c_{\text{deg}}}{\eta_c} \]

and between those two thresholds the optimal action is to do nothing. The width of that no-trade band is

\[ W \;=\; \mu\left(\frac{1}{\eta_d} - \eta_c\right) + 2\,c_{\text{deg}} \;>\; 0 . \]

The headline identity (from Part IV.11)

The marginal value of one more unit of battery capacity equals a sum of shadow prices:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathcal{V}^\star}{\partial E^{\max}} \;=\; \sum_t \Big(\underbrace{\bar\lambda_t}_{\text{arbitrage room}} \;+\; \underbrace{\psi^{\text{dn}}_t}_{\text{down-reserve room}}\Big) \]

Note what is absent: the upward-reserve multiplier \(\psi^{\text{up}}_t\). More capacity raises the ceiling, and a higher ceiling cannot relieve a floor — which is the entire subtlety we are working through.

The energy-headroom constraints (from Part III.17)

\[ \text{(EH-up)}\qquad S^+_t - S^{\min} \;\ge\; \frac{1}{\eta_d}\sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}^{\text{up}}} \tau_k\, u^k_t \] \[ \text{(EH-dn)}\qquad E^{\max} - S^+_t \;\ge\; \eta_c\sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}^{\text{dn}}} \tau_k\, u^k_t \]

\(\tau_k\) is the per-product duration requirement — the thing we still need to pin down from the ERCOT protocols, because it scales every one of these constraints.
